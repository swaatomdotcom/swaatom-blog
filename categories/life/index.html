Cabin fever

Here I am, stuck inside four walls in my room in Ta Khmau. There’s no official lockdown here, but it makes sense to limit social contact. I’m gonna escape for a while tomorrow though, and head across the river to visit the family. I think that I’m pretty well socially distanced riding a bicycle. I’m looking forward to it. I’m in two minds whether to take the ferry though (very crowded), or ride all the way up to the Prek Tamak bridge and back south again.

School's out

This time is different. I think that Cambodia’s amazing run of good luck has come to an end. The virus is with us now, and this time it appears to be here to stay. The genie ain’t gonna go back in the bottle this time. I hope I’m wrong. I would love to be wrong. It could’ve all been prevented. When will life return to normal? Ellen had only just started school when all of the covid closures kicked in last year.
