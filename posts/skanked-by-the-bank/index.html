Skanked by the bank

• 3 min read

Occasionally, something happens that brings out my inner Victor Meldrew. In this case, I think it was justified. I’ve had an account with ABA bank since I moved to Cambodia five years ago. This morning, the school contacted me to say that they couldn’t transfer my wages to my account. How very odd. So I got on the app, and saw that my account was in credit, everything okay - but hold on a minute, what’s this? The last transaction is a bank charge for $10, due to my account being charged for being dormant.

No way, lets contact customer services. No reply. Guess I have to go to the bank. So that’s what I did. After waiting for a short eternity, I finally got to talk to customer services staff (afraid I ended up getting a bit hot under the collar). It turns out that they’ve decided to deactivate my account because it was dormant. But I’ve been using the ATM at least 2 times a week for the last 2 years. So how?

Going through reams of printouts, the friendly staff diligently pointed out to me that my account had indeed been flagged as dormant.

THREE YEARS AGO

And with the same due care and attention, they have now (today) decided to steal $10 (sorry, I mean charges and fees) from me and deactivate the account. For something that happened three years ago. Three fucking years ago.

I’ve encountered sharp practice upon many occasions since I moved to Asia, but this is the first time I’ve been ripped off by an actual financial institution. $10 is not a huge sum of money. There are two issues that concern me more. The first is trust. I don’t think I would like to deposit a large sum of money with these guys. What other retrospective chicanery could they pull? The second issue is the inconvenience. I’ve had to wait for my money, and this has had a knock on effect for my family as well. I can’t wire any money until I receive my wages, and also my account needs to be active in order for me to make the transfer. Thank God that there aren’t any other issues such as medical bills to deal with this week. And all of this happened without the simple courtesy of a call to inform me of the situation. At least I found out in good time (by pure luck) so I was able to fix it before the weekend.

I guess that this covid is having a knock on effect for everyone. Maybe the bongtoms higher up in bank management aren’t getting enough tea money, so they are looking for ways to skank customers retrospectively. Or maybe it’s just down to the ever present incompetence you get to encounter here on a daily basis. Either way, corruption or incompetence, it isn’t good. Hope this isn’t happening to too many other people, but I would be surprised if I was the only victim of this. So if you bank with ABA, check that your accounts haven’t been suspended due to similar bullshit.

I’m gonna take a chill pill now. Relax. Take care and stay safe everyone.
